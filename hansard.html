03 — Findings

What the data tells us

Opposition parties naturally attack government record — so Labour's heavy focus on economy, health and housing reflects the standard playbook. But the scale of the gaps, and National's near-silence on environment, are notable.

Housing
3.4×
Labour mentions housing 3.4 times more often than National per 1,000 words. It's their most effective attack line against a government that promised to fix the housing crisis.
Immigration
National only
Immigration appears in National's statements but is virtually absent from Labour's releases in 2026 — Labour appears to be avoiding the topic ahead of the election.
Environment
10×
Labour mentions climate and environment ten times more often than National. Environmental policy has all but disappeared from National's public messaging.
Education
68×
Education is the biggest National advantage — 68 times more mentions per 1,000 words. Ministers Erica Stanford and others have made it a signature issue.
Economy
+5.4
Labour outpaces National on economic language by 5.4 mentions per 1,000 words — largely by attacking National's economic record: zero growth, unemployment, costs.
Crime & Costs
Neck & neck
Crime and living costs are the most contested ground — both parties engage roughly equally, suggesting neither has claimed clear ownership of these topics.

Methodology. 172 press releases and media statements published to national.org.nz and labour.org.nz between 1 January and 18 March 2026 were scraped and stored. Keyword matching used whole-word regex patterns across the full text and title of each statement. All frequencies are normalised per 1,000 words to account for the difference in volume between the two parties (National: 38,352 words; Labour: 27,652 words).

Keyword categories were defined before analysis to avoid selection bias. The scraper and analysis code are available on request. Data will be updated as new statements are published.
