Why language is data

Political parties make thousands of decisions about what to say and what to avoid. Press releases aren't incidental — they're deliberate signals about what a party wants voters to associate them with. NLP analysis turns that signal into something measurable.

The analysis in this collection uses keyword normalisation rather than raw counts, so a party that publishes more releases doesn't automatically dominate. It's about proportion: what share of a party's output is devoted to each topic, and how that compares across the spectrum.

6 Parliamentary parties analysed — ACT, Green, Labour, National, NZ First, Te Pāti Māori
8 Topic categories — housing, crime, climate, economy, health, education, foreign policy, social policy
