Geopolitics · Scenario model · 2026

If China moved on Taiwan — what would it mean for New Zealand?

A scrollable scenario model walking through nine economic channels — trade, tourism, education, housing, KiwiSaver, supply chains, inflation, military calculus, and the moment the decision lands — and then putting you in the room to make three of them. Built from real MFAT and RBNZ data. Interactive GDP outcome cards. Maps that move as you scroll.

An honest note: this is an audacious, overly comprehensive project attempted by one person and a laptop with broad but not deep knowledge of an extraordinarily complicated issue. The data is real. The economic channels are grounded. The scenario is hypothetical. The geopolitical modelling reflects public-source analysis, not classified intelligence. Treat it as a serious attempt to make something difficult legible — not as a forecast.

About me

Hi, I'm Harry Candlish

I'm a data analyst based in Wellington, New Zealand. I work across the full pipeline — collecting and cleaning data, building models, and presenting findings in a way that actually lands with the people who need to act on them.
