NZ Housing Data Story

The story behind NZ's housing crisis

Three datasets. One connected story — how interest rates, house prices, and housing deprivation are linked across New Zealand. All data sourced live from MySQL.

01

Interest rates hit a 15-year high

The RBNZ slashed the OCR to 0.25% during COVID — then raised it aggressively to 5.5% by 2023 to combat inflation. The fastest hiking cycle in New Zealand's history.

02

House prices surged — then corrected

Ultra-low rates fuelled a house price boom across every region. Auckland peaked near $1.2M median in 2022. As rates rose, prices fell — but remain far above pre-COVID levels.

03

Higher prices, higher deprivation

Cross-referencing house prices against Stats NZ deprivation data reveals a clear pattern — regions with higher prices don't always have higher deprivation rates. Northland and Gisborne stand out despite lower prices.
