How these scenarios are built

The Cabinet Room scenarios start from a real event or plausible trigger — then model what follows using publicly available economic data. The goal isn't prediction. It's structured thinking about decisions that are easy to defer until they can't be.

Every figure is sourced. Every assumption is stated. Where uncertainty is wide, ranges are used. Where the data doesn't exist, that gap is acknowledged rather than papered over.

  • 1

    Primary source data only. Stats NZ, MBIE, Reserve Bank, GBIF, UN Comtrade, Hansard. No commentary sites, no aggregated forecasts.

  • 2

    Explicit scenario structure. Each decision point has defined options with stated economic consequences — not narrative hand-waving.

  • 3

    Ranges, not point estimates. Central cases and tail risks are separated. Uncertainty is a feature, not a bug.

  • 4

    Interactive where it matters. Scrollytelling and decision-tree mechanics are used only when they genuinely add to the analysis.
